🔎 Phishing Red Flags to Watch For

🌐
URL Mismatch: The URL did not belong to the actual IoT manufacturer (e.g., localhost:5000 instead of cloud.manufacturer.com).
⏰
Artificial Urgency: Phrases like "expires today", "unauthorized access", or "act now" pressure you into hasty actions.
🎁
Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers: Free storage upgrades or surprise rewards are classic social-engineering baits.
🔓
No HTTPS / Certificate Warning: Legitimate portals use HTTPS. This simulation ran over plain HTTP on localhost.
📋
Generic Credentials Request: Legitimate services rarely ask you to re-enter passwords through an email link.

✅ How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify URLs — Always check that the domain matches the official manufacturer.
  • Enable 2FA — Use two-factor authentication on all IoT management portals.
  • Don't click email links — Navigate to the portal manually via your browser.
  • Update firmware — Keep IoT devices updated with the latest security patches.
  • Use a password manager — Auto-fill won't work on fake domains, alerting you.
  • Segment your network — Isolate IoT devices on a separate VLAN.
